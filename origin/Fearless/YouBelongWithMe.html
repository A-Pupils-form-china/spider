Writer(s): Liz Rose, Taylor Swift
Taylor Swift said about the background of this track, "I had overheard a friend of mine talking to his girlfriend and he was completely on the defensive, saying, 'I love you more than anything! Baby, I'm so sorry.' She was just yelling at him! I felt so bad for him at that moment. So, I came up with the first line..."
The meaning of this song was explained by Taylor Swift, who said, "This song is basically about wanting someone who is with this girl who doesn't appreciate him at all."
Lucas Till from "Hannah Montana: The Movie" played in the music video for the song.
The music video won the 2009 MTV Video Music Award for Best Female Video. It was there when Kanye West jumped on stage during Taylor Swift's acceptance speech to interrupt her and to say that Beyoncé's "Single Ladies (Put a Ring on It)" was one of the best videos of all time. Beyoncé's clip won Video of the Year later that night.
When Taylor Swift was asked by MTV News about the Kanye West's "moment" during 2009 MTV Video Music Award, she replied, "I was really excited, because I had just won the award. And then I was really excited because Kanye West was onstage. And then I wasn't so excited anymore after that."
Butch Walker made a banjo rendition of "You Belong With Me" and posted in online. When Taylor Swift heard it, she invited him to perform that version together on the 2010 Grammy telecast.
