Writer(s): Liz Rose, Hillary Lindsey, Taylor Swift
The fifth and final single from her second studio album. The song was written during the tour to promote her debut album, "Taylor Swift" (2006).
Taylor Swift explained the idea of this song to That's Country by saying, "This is a song about the fearlessness of falling in love. No matter how many break up songs you write, no matter how many times you get hurt, you will always fall in love again." She added, "I really was all by myself out on tour and I got this idea for a song about the best first date. I think sometimes when you're writing love songs, you don't write them about what you're going through at the moment, you write about what you wish you had."
On this song Taylor Swift wants the first kiss to be "flawless". She told CMT Radio, "That's what I love to write songs about. It's funny how you can mention one thing, like kissing, and have it be completely different in each song and have it mean a different thing."
The music video was composed of footage from the Fearless Tour performances and backstage.
"Fearless" debuted and reached number 9 on the US Billboard Hot 100 and was certified platinum by the Recording Industry Association of America.
