"Shake It Off" is the lead single from Taylor Swift's fifth album, "1989". The song was released on August 18, 2014.
"The message in the song is a problem I think we all deal with and an issue we deal with on a daily basis. We don't live just in a celebrity takedown culture, we live in a takedown culture. People will find anything about you and twist it to where it's weird or wrong or annoying or strange or bad. You have to not only live your life in spite of people who don't understand you, you have to have more fun than they do." - Taylor Swift.
The song debuted at #1 on the Billboard Hot 100, becoming Swift's second number one single in the US and the 22nd song to debut at number one in the chart's history. Interestingly, Mariah Carey's song of the same name went to #2 US in 2005.
The music video was premiered the same day the song was released. As of 2018, it is one of the most viewed videos on YouTube ever and also the most viewed by a female artist.
